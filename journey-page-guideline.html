Option 1
Identity Anchor
Their own words from onboarding, surfaced at the top. Quiet but high impact. Grounds everything that follows in their personal context and reminds them why they started.
open_field_response
Framing note: display verbatim. No paraphrase. The leader recognising their own voice is the whole point.
Option 2
Where you lead from
Two sources combined. The signature card (italicised quote) comes from their self-identified strength in onboarding Q3. The three strength cards come from the highest-scoring Compass subdomains by Current Confidence. The status badge ("Build on this" vs "Leverage and maintain") is drawn from the 2x2 quadrant logic based on combined confidence and priority scores. Three items shown max. Reflection prompt references strength overuse directly, the framing they already answered in Q3.
strength_overuse_signal confidence_score (top subdomains) growth_priority_score 2x2 quadrant classification
Tone note: all labels are invitations, not verdicts. "Build on this" suggests possibility. It does not declare a fixed identity.
Option 3
Where you're building
Low Confidence + High Growth Priority subdomains from Compass. These are the "Development focus" quadrant in the 2x2. Never labelled as weaknesses. Two items max to keep cognitive load low. The reflection prompt invites the leader to surface what feels most out of reach, and routes them to a relevant Practice if they engage.
confidence_score (low) growth_priority_score (high) 2x2 → development focus quadrant
The "Find a Practice" CTA links to Practices filtered by the relevant subdomain. Not a hard recommendation, an option.
Option 4
Across four domains
Replaces both radar charts. Four tiles, one per domain. Confidence expressed as a bar and a plain-language label: "Feeling settled", "Still building", "Early days". No percentages or numbers visible. The "On your mind" flag is drawn from onboarding Q1 (which domain was occupying their mental energy). This is the only section that surfaces domain-level data, not subdomain detail.
domain (onboarding Q1) confidence_ratings (onboarding Q2) avg confidence per domain (Compass)
Design note: use the average of onboarding Q2 analogue track position and average Compass subdomain confidence per domain for a more stable reading. Weight the Compass data more heavily once the user has completed it.
Option 5
Focus Map
All 23 subdomains bucketed into three visible groups using the 2x2 logic. The fourth quadrant ("Acknowledge, not the moment": low confidence, low priority) is intentionally omitted. There is no value in surfacing areas the leader has both low confidence in and low motivation to grow. Pill format keeps this scannable at a glance rather than requiring deliberate reading. This is the closest structural equivalent to the current radar, expressed in language not a chart.
2x2 quadrant classification (all 23 subdomains) confidence_score + growth_priority_score
Option 6
Journey So Far
Timeline arc with three nodes: start date, today, and a soft prompt toward the next Compass update. Two stat tiles: Practices completed and days since joining. Both are factual and grounded, not gamified. First login state: replace stats with a baseline-setting message ("This is your starting point. Everything from here is growth."). Post second Compass completion: add a delta card showing which specific subdomains have shifted and in which direction.
account created_at practices_completed (count) compass_completions (count + dates) confidence_delta (v1 vs v2+)
Future state: when a user completes their second Compass, this section gains a "What's shifted" card listing subdomains with notable confidence change. Threshold for "notable": suggest 15+ points of movement on a 100-point normalised scale to filter noise.
Option 7
How You Tend to Meet Challenge
Surfaces the orientation_score in plain human language. Four variants needed based on onboarding Q4 response. The card is small and positioned last, almost an aside. The note connects their orientation to the growth edges shown in Option 3, so the page ends with a gentle forward-looking observation rather than a summary.
orientation_score (A/B/C/D)
Four copy variants:
A (1.0): "You tend to treat hard moments as data, something to learn from."
B (0.5): "You tend to approach challenge as a mix, learning from some moments and pushing through others."
C (0.0): "You tend to focus on getting through hard moments and moving on."
D (0.5): "You told us it depends on the situation. That kind of self-awareness is itself a useful signal."
Future State
Values Anchor + Delta View
When the values tool is complete, it slots naturally between Option 1 (identity anchor) and Option 2 (strengths) as a second card in that section. The delta view in Option 6 becomes much richer once users have two or more Compass completions. Consider a separate "Growth" section at that point that lists specific subdomains with named movement, framed as recognition not measurement.
