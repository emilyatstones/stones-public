The playbook

Three steps. In this order.

Most people skip straight to building. That's why most ideas fail. Do these three steps in sequence and you'll be ahead of 90% of first-time builders.

Step 01

Validate

  • Pick the idea that excites you most from the list above.
  • Find 5 real potential buyers — a local coach, a club admin, a photographer, a small business owner. Not friends or parents.
  • Have a 15-minute conversation. Ask: what's annoying about this right now? What do you already pay for?
  • Check if anything similar already exists. Google it. Look on Gumroad and Product Hunt. If it does, that's good — it means demand is proven.
  • Test willingness to pay. Don't ask "would you use this?" Ask: "I'm building this — would you pay $20 for early access and give me feedback?"
A yes with money attached is a real yes. Anything else is just polite encouragement.
Step 02

Build

  • Map the workflow on paper first. What goes in? What comes out? What steps happen in between?
  • Build the simplest version that actually works. Do not add extra features yet.
  • Use Make.com or Zapier to connect a form to an AI model and send the output. No coding needed to start.
  • Test with 2–3 real users. Fix what breaks. Improve what's slow or confusing.
  • Package it so someone else can use it without your help.
The whole first version should cost under $50 NZD to build and run for a month.
Step 03

Sell

  • Direct outreach first. Your biggest advantage is that you're good with people. Message 20 local clubs, schools, or small businesses.
  • Offer a free trial in exchange for honest feedback and a testimonial if they like it.
  • Create a simple Gumroad page for one-time sales or subscriptions. No technical setup required.
  • Show the before and after on Instagram or TikTok. "I built this in a weekend" content gets real attention.
  • Your sports and school network is your warmest audience. Start there before going cold.
First goal: 5 paying customers. Not 500. Five tells you everything the research couldn't.
