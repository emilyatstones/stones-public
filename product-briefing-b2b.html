What your leaders get — the full product

Personal development — direction and goals
Leader-owned. HR-visible.
Compass
Self-assessment across 23 leadership subdomains
Each leader completes a Compass self-assessment across four domains — Self, People, Operational, and Strategic leadership — identifying where they feel confident and where they want to grow. Creates a personal development picture from day one.
What this means for you: Leaders arrive oriented. You get a cohort with clear development priorities rather than a group unsure where to start — higher engagement from the first session.
Goals
North Star goal-setting and milestone tracking
Leaders set their own development goals, connected to their Compass outcomes. Progress is tracked via milestones over time — making growth visible and accumulative rather than invisible and assumed.
What this means for you: Development is leader-driven and accountable. Leaders own their goals, which means you spend less time chasing engagement — and more time having the conversations that matter.
Skill-building — capability that sticks
Expert content. Behaviour change.
Practices
Behavioural learning loops for moments of need
When a leader is struggling with something specific — a performance conversation, managing self-doubt, navigating conflict — they enter a Practice. Expert video, a practical tool, a reflection exercise, and a follow-up nudge combine to build the skill through a real cycle of action and reflection.
What this means for you: Your leaders build capability in the moments they need it most, not months later in a workshop. The loop creates behaviour change, not just awareness — and that is what shows up in how they lead their teams day to day.
Learning
Curated expert content by ANZ practitioners
Structured expert content on specific leadership topics — curated by Stones' 26+ partner practitioners. Coaches, facilitators, and consultants with deep subject expertise create content grounded in real experience, not generic frameworks.
What this means for you: Your leaders are learning from practitioners who work with organisations like yours every day. Not off-the-shelf global content — ANZ-relevant, current, and grounded in what actually works in your context.
Stories
First-person accounts from leaders who have been there
Real leaders share what they navigated, what was hard, and what they learned. Specific, honest, and human in a way that expert content rarely is. Not prescriptive — lived experience that creates "someone else has been here" recognition.
What this means for you: Leadership development that feels real, not curated. Leaders stay engaged with content that reflects the complexity they actually experience — rather than disengaging from advice that feels sanitised or inapplicable.
Intelligence — staying ahead of what's changing
ANZ-relevant. Curated weekly.
Briefings
Topical intelligence from the world outside the organisation
Curated intelligence on what is shifting in the external landscape — employment law changes, workforce trend research, macroeconomic signals, global leadership findings. Each Briefing explains what has changed and what it means for ANZ leaders and their teams.
What this means for you: Your leaders are not caught off guard by shifts they should have seen coming. They arrive in conversations with you and their teams already informed — which changes the quality of those conversations.
Insights
Leadership thinking grounded in research
Short, evidence-based pieces on specific leadership topics — each grounded in research and giving leaders something specific and practical to reflect on. Warm, direct, and written for leaders who are busy and sceptical of generic advice.
What this means for you: Leaders develop the habit of thinking about their practice — not just managing the day-to-day. That reflective habit is one of the strongest predictors of sustained leadership growth.
Practical tools — ready to use, not just to read
Close the knowing-doing gap.
Toolkits
Templates, scripts, frameworks, and guides
The practical layer — conversation frameworks, planning templates, scripts for difficult situations, and guides that leaders pick up and use directly. Not content to read. Things to deploy in the actual moment they are needed.
What this means for you: Leaders move from knowing what they should do to actually doing it — which is where every development investment fails if the practical bridge is missing. Toolkits close that gap without adding overhead to you or your team.
Community — connection that costs you nothing to organise
Peer and expert connection. Built in.
Community Forum
Peer-to-peer and peer-to-expert discussion
A structured forum where leaders ask questions, share experience, and get responses from peers and Stones' expert partners. Moderated and purposeful — anchored to leadership topics, not a social feed. The antidote to the isolation that most leaders feel but rarely name.
What this means for you: Your leaders get the peer connection and external perspective that makes development stick — without you needing to organise cohort sessions, peer circles, or mentoring programmes. It's built into the product and it runs itself.

What you see as the organisation

Visibility without surveillance

Stones gives you the engagement data and thematic signals that tell a story to your leadership team — without exposing individual leader conversations or development content. Privacy is a design principle, not an afterthought.

Engagement data
You see that leaders are using it — and how often.
Cohort-level engagement rates, weekly active use, and content interaction data. Enough to report to the executive team and hold the investment case. No individual-level exposure of what a leader is working on.
Thematic signals
You see what your leaders are working on, not what they're struggling with.
Aggregate theme data shows which practice areas your cohort is most engaged with — giving you early signal on where capability gaps exist across the team, without breaching individual trust.
Impact reporting
You have something to show the board.
Regular engagement summaries with cohort-level data, development themes, and qualitative feedback. Structured to make the internal case easy — and to give finance and the executive team something they can understand and act on.
