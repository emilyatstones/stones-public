Ways to Contribute

Appendix — Stones for Partners

Stones

There is no single template for what partner contribution looks like. The format should suit your expertise and how your knowledge lands best. What matters is that what you contribute is useful to a leader in a real moment. Below is a guide to the contribution types available, what each involves, and the effort typically required.

Practices

Structured learning pathways — the core content type

Higher effort

A Practice is a structured learning pathway covering a specific leadership skill, behaviour, or situation. Built to last, returned to repeatedly by leaders over time. A Practice might take a leader through understanding a concept, reflecting on their own behaviour, and applying a tool or framework in a real moment.

Video Audio Written guide Reflection prompts Mixed format

Video modules

Standalone recorded expertise

Moderate effort

A recorded video — typically 5 to 20 minutes — where you share your perspective, explain a concept, walk through a framework, or respond to a question leaders frequently bring to you. Can sit inside a Practice or stand alone. Recorded remotely via Google Meet or Riverside; edited and produced by Stones.

Recorded remotely Stones-produced No studio required

Toolkits and frameworks

Practical resources for immediate use

Low to moderate

Something a leader picks up and uses right now. Checklists, decision guides, conversation scripts, planning templates, assessment tools, reframe cards. The format varies — what matters is that it makes a hard moment easier. Many partners already have these; contribution is as simple as sharing what you already use with clients.

PDF or document Checklist Script Template Decision guide

Leadership Exchange

Monthly live sessions with the Stones community

Low effort

A monthly live session hosted by Stones and led by a partner expert. Typically 60 minutes — part short input, part Q&A with leaders who are navigating the situations you know well. Topics are agreed in advance and tied to what's most relevant for leaders right now. Facilitated by Stones; you bring the knowledge.

Live via Zoom or Riverside Facilitated by Stones Recorded for library

Expert Q&As

Direct answers to live leader questions

Low effort

Short, focused responses to questions submitted by leaders in the Stones community. You answer from your area of expertise — in writing, on video, or live. This is the most conversational contribution type and often the one that generates the warmest leads, because leaders experience your thinking in response to their specific situation.

Written response Short video Live session

Written content

Blogs, micro-lessons, and newsroom pieces

Low effort

Shorter written pieces for leaders who need a direct answer, a quick reframe, or a focused piece of thinking on a timely topic. A blog post, a micro-lesson, a perspective piece — typically 400 to 800 words. Lightly edited by Stones and published under your name. A good starting point for new partners finding their contribution style.

Blog post Micro-lesson Perspective piece Topical update
01

Content brief

We work with you to identify the best format and topic for your first contribution, based on your area of expertise and what leaders in Stones need right now.

02

Shaping and production

The expertise is yours. The shaping, editing, and production is a shared process — we handle what needs to be handled so the output reflects your knowledge, not our workload.

03

Live and surfaced

Your content is published under your name and surfaced to leaders in the moments it is most relevant to what they are navigating.

Not sure where to start? Most partners begin with a written piece or a Leadership Exchange session — lower effort, immediate visibility, and a good way to see how your knowledge lands with the Stones community before investing in a Practice or video series.
