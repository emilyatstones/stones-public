What to include in each section
Use the numbered markers above as a reference. Each section is optional except your About bio.
1 Header — name, photo, and specialty Provided by you

Your profile photo and the name we display are set up when you onboard. Your specialty tag (the pill under your name) describes the broad area your work sits in, for example: Coaching & Growing People or Leadership Communication.

We will ask you for these when setting up your profile. A professional headshot works best — square crop, good light, clear face.

2 About 100–200 words

Structure: three short paragraphs works well.

  • Paragraph 1: Who you are and what you specialise in. Keep it specific. Name your organisation if you have one, your focus area, and who you work with.
  • Paragraph 2: Your approach or origin story. What shaped how you work? What do you believe about the problem you solve?
  • Paragraph 3 (optional): A lead-in to your bullet list. If you want to list the specific situations clients come to you for, open with a sentence like "Clients come to me when they need help with..." and follow with your list.
Tone Write in first person or third person consistently — either works. Sound like yourself, not like a bio you'd submit to a conference. Skip the generic credentials-speak: "passionate about helping leaders unlock their potential" tells a leader nothing. Say something specific that makes them feel understood.
What works "I work with leaders who are technically brilliant but find the people side of the job hard — difficult conversations, under-performers, team dynamics that are quietly draining everyone. I help them find language that's honest without being brutal, and approaches that move things forward without damaging what matters."
3 Credentials & experience 3–6 bullet points

List formal qualifications, professional accreditations, and notable past roles. Keep each item brief: institution or body, credential or role, that's it.

  • Formal qualifications (degree, diploma, certification)
  • Professional body memberships (ICF level, HRINZ, etc.)
  • Past roles worth naming (especially if they ground your expertise)
  • Published work or media if highly relevant
Tone Short and factual. No interpretation or explanation needed — the credential speaks for itself.
4 Modules (your Stones content) Generated automatically

This section populates automatically from the content you contribute to Stones. Each Practice or Story you create appears here as a card, with the title, duration, and lesson count pulled from your content.

The module title is the most important thing leaders see here. It should describe the specific leadership moment the content addresses, not the topic in the abstract.

Compare "Public speaking skills" — too generic. A leader scanning won't know if it's for them.

"What does your body language say about you?" — specific, makes the leader the subject.

We will work with you on your content title and brief when you submit your first module.

5 Get in touch Provided by you

Your email address, website URL, and LinkedIn profile. All three are optional but we recommend including at least one contact path.

Leaders who engage with your content will visit your profile. This is how they take the next step with you directly. Stones connects the dots; the relationship is yours to close.

6 Services Optional

List the services you offer outside of Stones — coaching packages, workshops, corporate programmes. Include pricing where you are comfortable sharing it. A price gives a leader enough to know whether to reach out, and saves time on both sides.

  • Service name (short, plain English)
  • One-line description of what it covers or who it is for
  • Price or price range (optional but recommended)
Tone Plain and direct. "1:1 coaching — $580 per session, 2–4 sessions typical" is exactly right.
